# Code of Conduct

RIFT is built and maintained by Nexstone Capital. We expect everyone who contributes — issues, pull requests, discussions — to follow this short code.

## What we expect

- **Be respectful.** Disagree with ideas, not with people. Assume good faith.
- **Be specific.** Vague complaints ("this is bad") move nothing forward. Concrete reproductions and proposals do.
- **Keep it technical.** This is a quant-trading toolkit. Discussion stays on the code, the math, the data, and the operational behavior — not personal politics, religion, or unrelated culture-war material.
- **No investment advice between contributors.** Discussing how a feature *works* is fine. Telling other contributors what to trade with their own capital is not. RIFT is software, not a tip line.

## What gets you removed

- Harassment, slurs, doxxing, sexual content, or threats — directed at any contributor, regardless of identity.
- Repeated bad-faith engagement (sealioning, brigading, sustained off-topic derailment) after a warning.
- Knowingly introducing malicious code, secret-stealing logic, or anything designed to harm other contributors or users.

## Reporting

Email `nexstone@proton.me` with the subject line `RIFT CoC report`. We will acknowledge within 72 hours and act on confirmed violations. Reports are confidential unless legal process requires otherwise.

For severe violations we may remove contributions, ban accounts from the repo, or report to platform abuse teams.

## Scope

This applies to all RIFT project spaces: the GitHub repository, issues, pull requests, and any official channels maintained by Nexstone.

This document is brief on purpose. If you need a more comprehensive framework, see the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/) — its norms apply here in spirit.
